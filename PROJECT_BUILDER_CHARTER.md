# Mission

Build an engineer capable of designing, building, and evolving software systems that solve meaningful problems.

The objective is not to master individual technologies.

The objective is to develop engineering judgment, first-principles thinking, and the ability to continuously learn throughout an engineering career.

---

# Purpose

Project Builder exists to document the complete journey from software fundamentals to AI Architecture.

The repository serves as both:

- an engineering apprenticeship, and
- a public record of continuous learning.

Every project, document, and engineering decision should contribute toward this purpose.

---

# Guiding Principles

Project Builder is governed by the following principles.

- First principles before syntax.
- Engineering before frameworks.
- Problems before technologies.
- Understanding before implementation.
- Quality over speed.
- Documentation is part of the product.
- Build deliberately.
- Never stop asking "Why?"

---

# Responsibilities

## Builder

- Learn deliberately.
- Build consistently.
- Document honestly.
- Accept feedback.
- Share knowledge.
- Continuously improve engineering judgment.

---

## Technical Director

- Teach engineering rather than syntax.
- Introduce abstractions only after establishing the problems they solve.
- Encourage first-principles thinking.
- Preserve the long-term vision of Project Builder.
- Maintain high engineering standards.
- Continuously adapt the apprenticeship as the Builder grows.

---

# Success

Project Builder succeeds when:

- meaningful software is built,
- engineering judgment matures,
- knowledge is shared,
- and future engineers benefit from the journey.

Success is measured by long-term impact rather than short-term achievements.

---

# Charter Status

This Charter is intended to remain stable.

Operational details, roadmaps, current progress, and learning plans belong in other repository documents.

The Charter changes only when the fundamental purpose or philosophy of Project Builder evolves.
